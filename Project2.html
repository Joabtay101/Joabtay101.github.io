Caerdydd Dragon - A Puzzle Platformer

Overview

Caerdydd Dragon is a 2D puzzle platformer inspired by the cultural heritage of Wales. The game features the iconic Welsh dragon as the protagonist, navigating levels set within Cardiff Castle and collecting daffodils along the way. Developed in Unity (C#), the game emphasizes accessibility, smooth movement mechanics, and a well-balanced difficulty curve, while showcasing a strong sense of place and identity tied to its setting. The project was a team effort, with all four members contributing to game mechanics, story, and overall design.

Project Goals

  • Engaging Gameplay: Deliver an intuitive yet challenging experience with accessible mechanics.
  • Cultural Representation: Capture the essence of Welsh culture through art, themes, and level design.
  • Technical Execution: Implement polished platforming mechanics, responsive controls, and smooth camera handling.
  • Replayability: Encourage player engagement through collectibles and refined mechanics.

Key Features

  • Smooth Movement & Camera Mechanics: Designed for accessibility and tested for intuitive control.
  • Progressive Difficulty & Level Design: Two levels, each introducing new mechanics.
  • Unique Gameplay Elements: Wind Zones, Collectibles, and Enemy Interactions.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine & Language: Unity (C#)
  • Git Workflow: Master branch for final submission, development branch for ongoing work, and feature branches for isolated implementations.
  • Key Algorithms: Singleton Pattern, Input Handling, UI Animation System, Camera Control, and Enemy AI.

Playtesting & Feedback

20 playtesters provided feedback on accessibility, mechanics comprehension, and overall enjoyment. Key insights:

  • 80% found controls easy to grasp.
  • 95% enjoyed jumping onto enemies.
  • 80% found the Wind Zone engaging but challenging.
  • Overall, the Welsh theme was well-received, with most players rating it 4-5/5.

Evaluation & Conclusion

From a playtesting point of view the game received positive feedback, achieving an overall score of 4/5 across key metrics. From a creators point of view we successfully met the objectives we had oringinally set of accessibility, cultural representation, and engaging gameplay, and as a group we were very happy with the result in the limited time frame we had. Therefore although we saw room for improvement if we were to work on the game down the line, we saw the conclusion of the project as an overall success!
