Caerdydd Dragon - A Puzzle Platformer

Overview

Caerdydd Dragon is a 2D puzzle platformer inspired by the cultural heritage of Wales. The game features the iconic Welsh dragon as the protagonist, navigating through levels set within Cardiff Castle, collecting daffodils along the way. The game emphasizes accessibility, smooth movement mechanics, and an engaging difficulty curve while showcasing a strong sense of place and identity tied to its setting.

Project Goals

  • Engaging Gameplay: Provide an intuitive yet challenging experience.
  • Cultural Representation: Capture Welsh culture through environment design.
  • Technical Execution: Develop a polished platforming system.
  • Replayability: Encourage players to return through collectibles and mechanics.

Key Features

  • Smooth movement & camera mechanics with intuitive controls.
  • Progressive difficulty with two distinct levels.
  • Wind Zones for aerial movement and strategic platforming.
  • Collectibles and enemy interactions designed for engagement.

Technical Specifications

  • Git Workflow: Master, Development, and Feature branches.
  • Key Algorithms: Singleton pattern, state-based input handling.
  • Camera System: Dynamic tracking and cinematic triggers.
  • Enemy AI: Triggerable actors with a shared base class.
  • Documentation: Doxygen-generated class documentation available.

Playtesting & Feedback

  • 20 playtesters assessing accessibility and mechanics comprehension.
  • 80% found controls easy to grasp.
  • 95% found jumping onto enemies fun.
  • 80% found Wind Zones enjoyable but some struggled with the difficulty.
  • Adjustments made for better tutorialisation and balance.

Evaluation & Conclusion

The project successfully met its objectives of accessibility, cultural representation, and engaging gameplay. Feedback scores averaged 4/5, with refinements planned for future iterations.
